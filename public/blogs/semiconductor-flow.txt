🏁 Checkpoint! Here's more information:

![Semiconductor Flow Infographic](./semiconductor_flow.png)

1. **Specification**: Defining the functional requirements, performance goals, power constraints and other specifications for the chip, based on customer/market requirements. This is a really important step, requiring lots of experience.

2. **Architecture**: Designing the high-level structure of functional blocks and their interactions in the chip while evaluating options and trade-offs to meet the specifications efficiently. Power consumption, performance, scalability are considered.

3. **Design**: Creating a logic implementation based on the architecture in Verilog/VHDL to implement complex algorithms, data paths and control logic.

4. **Verification**: Developing testbenches and test cases to verify the functionality of the design against the specifications by simulation-based testing using tools like Synopsis VCS or Cadence Xcelium. Functional, formal, digital, analog and many methods to prove the design's accuracy.

5. **Synthesis**: Converting the high-level logic into a gate-level netlist that represents the hardware implementation. Optimizing the design for area, power, and timing using synthesis tools like Synopsys Design Compiler or Cadence Genus.

6. **Place & Route (P&R)**: Determining the physical locations of chip components, "floor planning" to allocate space for functional blocks and I/O interfaces, and routing the connections between components efficiently.

7. **Fabrication**: Manufacturing the physical chip using fabrication processes such as photolithography, etching, and doping. Usually outsourced to foundries such as TSMC/Samsung.

8. **Testing**: Performing wafer-level testing and packaged-chip testing to ensure functionality and reliability, analyzing test results and identifying any defects or failures for further investigation.

9. **Debugging**: Identifying and diagnosing issues discovered during testing using tools such as logic analyzers, waveform viewers, and debug probes to trace and analyze signals.

10. **Integration**: Integrating the chip into a larger product environment and interfacing the chip with other system components like processors, memory and peripherals. Performing system-level validation and testing to ensure interoperability.

11. **Deployment**: Preparing the chip for mass production and distribution, coordinating with supply chain partners for sourcing materials and components, and finally shipping the product to the market.

Each phase represents thousands of developer hours and is critical to ensuring the reliability of the tech we use every day!
